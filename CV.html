Florida Institute of Technology — Research Assistant

Melbourne, FL, May 2025 — August 2025

I implemented significant updates to preserve institutional knowledge, including reorganizing and expanding project documentation across Github repositories and Overleaf, and authored intructional sections on topics such as Git usage and working within high-performance computing environments. Alongside this, I streamlined the project's data storage by converting large simulation output into a modern, compressed file format. I also developed Python tools to automate performance checks, evaluate model output and find hidden errors, and efficiently work with large parameter spaces. In the course of this work, the team was investigated a theoretical mismatch in the calculation of a coefficient commonly seen in the scientific literature. Presentations and Teaching Experience

Quantum Information and CHSH Lectures — View Presentation Materials

Oral Presentations, Florida Institute of Technology, April 2025

I gave three separate hour-long lectures on Quantum Information and the CHSH inequality. Each of these lectures were given to groups of about 5-20 students, each hosted by Dr. Souvik Das. The first two lectures were given in two sections, the first on providing the necessary background knowledge in quantum information and the second expanded on the previous lecture, and showed all of the related derivations of the CHSH inequality, Tsirelson's bound, and exhibited code running on IBM hardware for empirical evidence of the CHSH experiment. The final lecture was to a larger group and was a condensed version of the two more focused lectures, focusing on the basics of quantum information and introducing the CHSH experiment.

Neural Networks for Parameter Estimation of Orphan Afterglows in Rubin and Roman Data — View Presentation

I gained invaluable insight from my presentation and attendance at the conference. Additionally, attending ASROC 2025 was my first experience internationally. This international experience broadened my understanding of how diverse scientific communities collaborate and I gained a deeper perspective on how astronomy unites scientists worldwide toward a common quest for knowledge.
